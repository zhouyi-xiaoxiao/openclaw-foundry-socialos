# SocialOS 5-Minute VC Script

Hi everyone.

The problem I care about is not that people do not have enough tools. It is that the signals that matter most in modern work get fragmented.

You meet someone at an event. You have a meaningful conversation. You save a note, maybe a voice memo, maybe a screenshot, maybe a business card. Later, you want to follow up, turn that moment into content, and understand what it meant for your work or your life. But the person, the context, the draft, and the self-reflection all end up in different places.

That is the problem SocialOS is built for.

SocialOS is a local-first relationship and identity operating system.

It takes raw notes, voice memos, screenshots, and encounters, and turns them into structured people memory, event context, self signals, and platform-native draft packages.

The wedge is what I call high-context people: founders, researchers, creators, BD operators, and community builders whose work runs on relationships. These are people who are constantly meeting others, absorbing context, generating ideas, and translating lived experience into follow-up, content, and judgment.

What SocialOS does differently is close one loop.

It starts in a single chat-first Workspace. I can begin with one natural note instead of filling out a form. I can write that I met someone after a talk, what we discussed, what stood out, and what I want to remember. SocialOS turns that into structured people and event memory.

Then I can recall it later with fuzzy context, not just exact search. I do not need the precise name or event title. I can search from the shape of the memory, and the system can bring the right person or interaction back to me.

Once that moment becomes worth expressing, SocialOS can generate a clean set of platform-native drafts around the same event. Today that means one draft each for LinkedIn, X, and Instagram in English, and one draft each for Zhihu, Rednote, WeChat Moments, and WeChat Official Accounts in Chinese.

And importantly, the product does not pretend everything should be fully automated. The first version is trust-first. Drafts move into a queue and prepare a handoff. The system helps me organize, validate, and package the work, but it does not auto-post on my behalf.

Finally, all of this feeds into Self Mirror. That means the product is not only helping me remember people or publish content. It is also helping me reflect on what is actually happening in my work and life, based on evidence. Not a personality quiz. Not vague self-help labels. Evidence-backed reflection grounded in real interactions and real events.

That is why I think SocialOS is a new category.

Most tools solve only one layer. CRM tools track people, but they do not understand expression. Social tools help generate posts, but they do not understand relationships. Reflection tools help you journal, but they are disconnected from the real social graph of your life.

SocialOS combines all three. It is Relationship OS, Content OS, and Self OS in one loop.

And the reason I believe this is credible is that it is built around real operational boundaries, not just a nice demo story.

It is local-first because the most valuable data here is personal: private notes, screenshots, relationship context, and self signals. It stays loopback-only and dry-run by default because trust has to be earned before automation can expand. Under the surface, the system links people and events in a structured way, so memory and recall are not just loose text search. And the multi-agent runtime is there as enabling infrastructure so capture, memory, reflection, validation, and publish handoff can stay narrow, safer, and more explainable.

For DoraHacks specifically, I keep the public proof layer separate from the interactive runtime. The live demo remains localhost-only, while the deck, bounty hub, and proof pages are published as a stable read-only site that judges can revisit after the video.

What matters most is that the core loop already works today. A user can capture naturally, recall with fuzzy context, generate seven platform-native drafts, move through a trust-first queue, and end with evidence-backed reflection.

From here, the next unlocks are clear. First, a review-first import inbox, so users can bring in their real historical notes, screenshots, business cards, and chat fragments without rebuilding their world from scratch. Second, multi-entity capture, so one raw note can produce multiple people and interactions. Third, stronger assisted publishing and better mention suggestions, especially for professional networks like LinkedIn.

So the ask right now is simple.

I am looking for design partners and intros to high-context users who feel this problem deeply. People whose work depends on relationships, context, expression, and self-awareness, and who know that these should not live in separate tools forever.

If that sounds like you, or someone you can introduce me to, I would love to talk.

Thank you.
