# SocialOS 5-Minute Pitch

## 0:00-0:30 Problem Hook
People, context, content, and self-understanding drift apart.

You meet someone at an event, talk about something important, promise yourself to follow up, maybe turn it into a post, and then the signal disappears across chats, notes, screenshots, and memory.

## 0:30-1:10 One-Line Product Definition
SocialOS is a local-first relationship and identity operating system.

It turns raw notes, voice memos, screenshots, and encounters into:
- people memory
- event context
- self signals
- platform-native draft packages

## 1:10-2:40 Product Demo Arc
Show only this product loop:
1. `Workspace`
   Capture one natural note about meeting someone.
2. `Contacts` or `Logbook`
   Recall the person or event with fuzzy natural language.
3. `Drafts`
   Show the seven standard platform drafts for the event.
4. `Queue`
   Show assisted handoff rather than risky auto-posting.
5. `Mirror`
   Show daily or weekly reflection grounded in evidence.

## 2:40-3:30 Why It Is Different
Most tools only solve one layer:
- CRM tools track people
- social tools generate posts
- journaling tools reflect on the self

SocialOS closes the loop between all three:
- Relationship OS
- Content OS
- Self OS

## 3:30-4:15 Why The System Is Credible
The product is credible because it is built around real operational boundaries:
- model-first capture and recall
- graph-backed links between people and events
- local-first and loopback-only by default
- evidence-backed mirror instead of vague personality labels
- assisted publishing first, not reckless automation

The multi-agent/OpenClaw layer is there to make the loop work safely, not to be the headline.

## 4:15-5:00 Close + Roadmap
The next layer is clear:
- review-first import inbox for real historical data
- multi-entity capture so one note can produce multiple people and interactions
- LinkedIn mention suggestions and better assisted publishing

The product already shows the loop. The next phase makes it easier for real users to onboard without rebuilding their world from scratch.
