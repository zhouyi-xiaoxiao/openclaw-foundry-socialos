# SocialOS VC Deck Spec

This document is the curated source of truth for the public VC deck served from `/deck`.

## Audience
- Early-stage VCs
- Design partners
- High-context operators who can open user channels

## Narrative Rules
- Lead with user pain, not architecture.
- Lead with category and loop, not feature inventory.
- Mention multi-agent and OpenClaw only as enabling architecture.
- Keep every slide to one core idea.
- Keep the closing ask to `design partners + intros`, not a priced round.

## Slide Order
1. Problem
2. What SocialOS is
3. Who it is for
4. Product loop
5. What works today
6. Why it is different
7. Why this is credible
8. What I want now

## Slide Headlines
1. `People, context, content, and self-understanding drift apart.`
2. `A local-first relationship and identity operating system.`
3. `Built for high-context people, and already grounded in a real network.`
4. `One loop: capture, recall, express, hand off, reflect.`
5. `The core loop already works.`
6. `It combines Relationship OS, Content OS, and Self OS in one loop.`
7. `Trust-first product, real proof, expandable architecture.`
8. `Design partners and intros for the next unlock.`

## Slide Intent
### 1. Problem
- Establish fragmentation as the core pain.
- Show that the user problem is continuous, not one isolated workflow.
- Visual source: conceptual problem cards, not architecture.

### 2. What SocialOS is
- Give the category definition.
- Show one strong product screenshot.
- Visual source: `socialos/docs/evidence/socialos-demo-step01.png`

### 3. Who it is for
- Define the wedge clearly.
- Explain “high-context people” in plain language.
- Show that the demo is seeded with real named relationships, not placeholders.
- Visual source: network cluster cards with named contacts, not a market-size chart.

### 4. Product loop
- Show the operating loop clearly:
  - Workspace
  - Contacts / Logbook
  - Drafts
  - Queue
  - Mirror
- Visual source: simple flow diagram.

### 5. What works today
- Establish proof that this is more than a concept.
- Mention real named contacts, fuzzy recall, linked people + events, drafts, queue handoff, daily/weekly mirror.
- Visual source: `socialos/docs/evidence/socialos-demo-step04.png`, `socialos/docs/evidence/socialos-demo-step08.png`

### 6. Why it is different
- Position SocialOS against isolated tools without a long comparison matrix.
- Emphasize the combined loop:
  - Relationship OS
  - Content OS
  - Self OS

### 7. Why this is credible
- Use proof, not hype:
  - local-first trust boundary
  - model-first capture
  - graph-backed linking
  - multi-agent runtime as enabling architecture
- Visual source: proof cards driven by `DECK_STATUS.json`

### 8. What I want now
- Explain what support unlocks:
  - design partners
  - intros to high-context users and believers
  - next product unlocks: real-data onboarding, multi-entity capture, LinkedIn mention suggestions

## Visual Source Rules
- Use curated evidence already in the repo.
- Do not use localhost-only links in public deck mode.
- Local rehearsal URLs may appear only in rehearsal mode or speaker notes.
- Keep the deck self-contained and repo-native.

## Out Of Scope
- Do not turn the deck into a general product dashboard.
- Do not show deep operator/Studio internals in the main story.
- Do not add raw local run logs or volatile runtime state.
- Do not auto-regenerate slide copy from scripts.
