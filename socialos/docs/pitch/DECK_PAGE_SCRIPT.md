# SocialOS Deck Page Script

This script follows the `/deck` page order and is written to be read directly or semi-directly live. Each page includes a speaker version and a clear advance cue.

## Slide 1 · Problem
Suggested read:

“People, context, content, and self-understanding drift apart.

High-context people do not fail because they lack tools. They fail because one meaningful conversation gets fragmented across chats, notes, screenshots, content drafts, and memory. You meet someone important, promise yourself to follow up, maybe even want to turn it into a post, and two days later the signal is gone.”

Advance when:
- Move forward after “the signal is gone.”

## Slide 2 · What SocialOS Is
Suggested read:

“SocialOS is a local-first relationship and identity operating system.

It takes one messy daily input and turns it into structured people memory, event context, platform-native draft packages, and a daily or weekly mirror. The point is not another dashboard. The point is one calm operating surface where relationships, content, and reflection stay connected.”

Advance when:
- Move forward after “stay connected.”

## Slide 3 · Real Network
Suggested read:

“The wedge is high-context people: founders, organisers, operators, researchers, and community builders whose work runs on remembering people and context.

And this demo is no longer seeded with placeholders. It already carries real named relationships across London, Bristol, Chengdu, and San Francisco, from hackathon organisers like Minghan Xiao and Shafi Maahe, to legal and cross-border contacts like Candice Tang, to academic and industry contacts like James Wu, Daniel D’Andrea, Alan Champneys, and Michele Barbour.”

Advance when:
- Move forward after saying “no longer seeded with placeholders” and naming at least three people.

## Slide 4 · Product Loop
Suggested read:

“The product story is one loop: capture, recall, express, hand off, reflect.

On screen, the loop is simple: Workspace, Contacts or Logbook, Drafts, Queue, then Mirror. In other words, one input can move from capture, to memory, to expression, to a trust-first handoff, and finally into evidence-backed reflection.”

Advance when:
- Move forward after walking through all five nodes in the loop.

## Slide 5 · What Works Today
Suggested read:

“This is already more than a concept deck.

Today the system already supports real named contacts, linked identities, linked events, fuzzy recall, seven platform-native drafts, trust-first queue handoff, and daily or weekly mirror summaries. On screen, I can now literally show Contacts, Drafts, and Queue from the same seeded dataset. So the story is not ‘imagine if this existed.’ The story is ‘the core loop already works, and now it needs better onboarding and more daily use.’”

Advance when:
- Move forward after “the core loop already works.”

## Slide 6 · Why It Is Different
Suggested read:

“Most tools solve one layer only.

CRMs track people. AI writing tools generate posts. Journaling tools reflect on the self. SocialOS connects all three into one loop: Relationship OS, Content OS, and Self OS. That is why it feels like an operating system rather than a single workflow tool.”

Advance when:
- Move forward after explaining the three-layer structure.

## Slide 7 · Why This Is Credible
Suggested read:

“The product is credible because the trust boundary is real.

It is local-first, loopback-only by default, and staged around safe rehearsal instead of reckless automation. The multi-agent layer matters, but only as enabling architecture: capture, memory, linking, validation, and publishing are different jobs. The user still experiences one calm product surface.”

Advance when:
- Move forward after “one calm product surface.”

## Slide 8 · Ask
Suggested read:

“What I want now is not to force a priced round story.

I want design partners and intros to high-context users who already live across relationships, notes, content, and reflection every day. The next unlock is real-data onboarding, multi-entity capture, and making this product disappear even more naturally into daily life.”

Advance when:
- Pause after the final sentence and move into Q&A.

## Slide 9 · Appendix · Claw for Human
Suggested read:

"Claw for Human is the most judge-facing lens. The important point is not that agents exist. It is that the user sees one guided relationship workspace with visible proof and a real trust boundary."

## Slide 10 · Appendix · Animoca Bounty
Suggested read:

"Animoca is the persistence slide. SocialOS already keeps identities, interactions, events, and agent lanes linked over time, so the system behaves like a long-horizon memory layer, not a one-task chatbot."

## Slide 11 · Appendix · Human for Claw
Suggested read:

"Human for Claw is Buddy mode: four safe tasks, simple framing, no risky publish lane, and visible boundaries."

## Slide 12 · Appendix · Z.AI General
Suggested read:

"Z.AI General is shown honestly. The provider-routing surface and proof path are already in place. In this environment, GLM is shown in fallback mode until a live key is configured."

## Slide 13 · Appendix · AI Agents for Good
Suggested read:

"AI Agents for Good reframes the same memory and follow-through system as community support. SDG triage labels the impact context, and then SocialOS carries that into relationship memory and next actions."
